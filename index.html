After high school I had no intention to go to college, so I spent a year traveling around my home country, Chile. It was during these travels that I focused on reading and reflecting upon how to proceed with life.

While on a bus traveling in the northern deserts of Chile, I had an epiphany after observing true third-world poverty when I asked myself why and how countries develop and grow out of poverty. I had resolved to study Economics so that I could work to pull people out of poverty.

After college I was eager to make my impact on the world and found myself working at the US House of Representatives. Here I quickly learned that change doesn't happen from working as a low level staffer nor as a senior staffer, and nearly immediately resolved to find a better opportunity.

I stumbled across an opening at a bank for corporate management training program and found my place in helping to small businesses, which then fuels economic growth, job and wealth creation.

I now seek to use financial technologies, like Blockchain, so that small business in Latin America may also benefit, therefore helping to raise poor countries out of poverty.
